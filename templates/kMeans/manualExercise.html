6. Variance Comparison

Variance measures how far the data points are from their assigned centroids. It helps evaluate how compact the clusters are.

In this exercise, the variance was calculated as the square of the distance between each data point and its assigned centroid. Using the formula:

Variance = ∑d^2

For each data point, the distance to the assigned centroid was squared,por example:

For P2 in iteration 1, whose cluster is C1, the distance to the centroid of that cluster was taken and squared. d(C1) = (5.10)^2 Variance = 26.01

and then all values were summed to obtain the total variance for each iteration. The result is:

Iteration Variance
Iteration 15455,00
Iteration 23281,47
Iteration 13138,18

By comparing the variances of each iteration, we can observe that the variance decreases with each iteration. This demonstrates that the algorithm improves clustering by reducing the distance between the points and their centroids.

The decrease in variance across iterations indicates that the K-Means algorithm is converging and producing more stable clusters.

7. Results Interpretation

The K-Means algorithm allows the identification of three distinct groups based on the variables of study hours and grades.

As a result of each cluster, this represents:

  • Cluster 1: Represents students with low study hours and low grades.
  • Cluster 2: Represents students with moderate study hours and average grades.
  • Cluster 3: Represents students with high study hours and high grades.

The variance decreased across iterations, indicating that the data points became closer to their respective centroids.

The centroids showed minimal changes between the last iterations, indicating that convergence between the data was achieved.

Overall, the data were successfully grouped into meaningful clusters, demonstrating the effectiveness of K-Means in identifying patterns within the dataset.
